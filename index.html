THE PROBLEM

Brands want to stand out, not see what their rivals are doing.

Our sellers needed a way to walk advertisers through every in-app gaming format we offer — without showing real brand executions. The minute a Pepsi creative shows up in a Coke pitch, the conversation is over.

CONSTRAINT

No brand assets, no logos, no real campaign creative.

OUTPUT

Twelve short loops, one per format, ready for any deck.

AUDIENCE

InMobi's global sales team and the advertisers they meet.

SHELF LIFE

Long. Format-first creative outlives any campaign cycle.

The format is the product. The brand is the variable.
So we made the format the star — and let it look like nothing the advertiser has ever seen before. — ART DIRECTION NOTE
THE AESTHETIC

Why Y2K works for gaming.

Gaming and Y2K share a vocabulary: arcade cabinets, CRT scanlines, chunky pixel type, neon, vaporwave grids, the sticker-pop of late-90s Memphis design. It feels like play before anyone says "play". And it doesn't look like any brand on Earth — which is exactly what the brief asked for.

WORLD 01 / TWO
VAPORWAVE

Deep space, perspective grids, gradient orbs, neon outlines. Used for action and precision formats — Catcher, Goalie, Shooter, Master Collector, Shape Tracer, Tic Tac Toe.

WORLD 02 / TWO
MEMPHIS POP

Hot pink, mint, checkerboards, chunky outlined type, sticker stars and slime. Used for casual, lucky, and word-play formats — Slots, Spin D Wheel, Puzzle, Mix & Match, Guess D Word, Drag Master.
